In a harrowing escalation of hostilities, Iran unleashed a storm upon Israel like never before, igniting a geopolitical firestorm that reverberated across the globe. The dawn of April 2024 marked the beginning of Operation True Promise One, an unprecedented launch of over 300 missiles and drones targeting critical Israeli installations.

From the heart of Tehran, these projectiles raced towards the Negev desert, striking Nevatim Air Base and Ramon Air Base. This bold assault constituted the very first direct attack on Israeli soil, shattering the long-held belief of invulnerability.

Just months later, in October, Operation True Promise Two unleashed an onslaught of roughly 180 ballistic missiles, again targeting Nevatim and the critical Tel Nof airbase, showcasing Iran's unwavering resolve to destabilize its adversary.

June 2025 brought the Twelve-Day War, with Operation True Promise Three launching hundreds of missiles aimed at Israel's nuclear and military facilities, including the highly secretive sites near Dimona and Tel Aviv. The stakes had never been higher.

In February and March 2026, a counter-effort commenced as Iran prepared retaliatory strikes against US-Israeli operations, heralding an era of uncertainty for the Middle East.

As the tides shift and the stakes rise, the world watches closely. The face of the Middle East has changed forever, with impacts yet to unfold in this ongoing saga of conflict.
